Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

there is a strong positive correlation between health coverage and the percentage of households in poverty by state

which is further tested by compparing the same health coverage percentage versus median household income, with a large negative correlation

another observation even more disturbing, is that as percentage of people with obesity by state increase, there is also a increase in the lack of health coverage, so states with lower average income, and high percentage of people in poverty, will tend to have higher levels of people in obesity with a higher percentage of lacking of healthcare services.
